Industries already using AR

AR has been mainstream for years—Prime Rig focuses on turning it into practical business workflows.
Retail leaders

“Try before you buy” is proven

Major retailers have already trained customers to expect AR previews—seeing products in 3D and in their space. Prime Rig packages that same behavior into fast, shareable, embedded experiences.

  • Apple: AR Quick Look lets websites show products in 3D and AR.
  • Amazon: “View in Your Room” enables in-space previews.
  • Best Buy: AR Viewer helps customers check size/fit at home.
Social platforms

AR is familiar to millions

Snapchat and Meta built massive AR adoption through camera effects—making AR feel normal, fun, and intuitive. Prime Rig brings that accessibility into business contexts.

  • Snapchat: Lenses popularized AR camera experiences.
  • Meta: Spark enabled AR effects across Instagram/Facebook.
Why AR scales

Runs on-device, delivered like media

Unlike cloud-hosted generative AI, WebAR rendering typically happens on the user’s device (their camera + GPU), while assets are served like any other website media. That means quick delivery, fewer moving parts, and no need for large inference clusters just to display a product.

Prime Rig focuses on optimizing models, loading fast, and embedding AR where customers already are: websites, QR codes, and product pages.

Origin story

How Prime Rig started

Prime Rig started from a problem I kept seeing: businesses have great products, spaces, and experiences—but customers can’t always picture them. When someone can’t visualize size, fit, or detail, decisions slow down and trust drops.

The tech already exists—3D viewers, WebAR delivery, lightweight embedding—so Prime Rig focuses on putting pre-existing AR technology into practical business applications that are easy to deploy and easy to share.

The goal: make AR feel useful, not gimmicky—so it becomes a repeatable part of how businesses sell, explain, and build trust.
